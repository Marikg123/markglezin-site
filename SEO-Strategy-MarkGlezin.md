# SEO Strategy: markglezin.com

## What's Already Implemented (Technical Foundation)

The following SEO elements have been added to the site:

**Meta tags on all 4 pages** with keyword-rich titles and descriptions targeting collectors, galleries, and art fairs. Each page has unique Open Graph and Twitter Card tags so artwork displays beautifully when shared on social media.

**Schema.org structured data** on the home page (Person schema with artist info, location, social links) and works page (CollectionPage schema). This helps Google understand the site is an artist portfolio.

**sitemap.xml** with image extensions, listing all pages including category sections. **robots.txt** pointing to the sitemap.

**Image optimization** already in place: all paintings converted to WebP, max 1800px, quality 88 (344MB originals down to ~13MB). Native lazy loading on gallery images.

---

## Immediate Next Steps (Week 1-2)

### 1. Google Search Console Setup
Go to search.google.com/search-console, add markglezin.com as a property. Verify ownership by adding a meta tag or uploading an HTML file. Submit the sitemap URL: `https://www.markglezin.com/sitemap.xml`. This is the single most important step for getting indexed.

### 2. Google Business Profile
Create a Google Business Profile at business.google.com. Category: "Artist" or "Art Studio." Add studio address in Tel Aviv, phone, website, hours, and photos. This is critical for local searches like "painter in Tel Aviv" and gives the site authority signals.

### 3. Art Marketplace Profiles
Create profiles on these platforms (each gives a high-authority backlink plus direct collector traffic):

**Artsy** (artsy.net) — The most prestigious. Apply for artist listing. This is where serious collectors and galleries browse.

**Saatchi Art** (saatchiart.com) — Large collector audience. List available works with prices. Good for direct sales.

**Artspan** (artspan.com) — Commission-free platform for artists.

**AskART** (askart.com) — Database used by galleries and auction houses for artist research.

On each profile, link back to markglezin.com. These backlinks from high-authority art domains are extremely valuable for SEO.

## Google Image Search (20-35% of Art Traffic)

Image search is the biggest traffic opportunity for visual artists. People search Google Images for "figurative paintings," "oil portrait," etc.

**What's already done:** All images have alt text from the painting title and WebP format for fast loading.

**Additional improvements to consider:**

Add more descriptive alt text to key paintings. Instead of just the title, include medium and subject: "Oil on panel portrait painting of a woman in red, 90x70cm, by Mark Glezin" performs better than just "Commission portrait."

The image sitemap in sitemap.xml helps Google discover and index all gallery images.

File names already contain medium and dimensions, which helps.

## Content Recommendations

### About Page
The About page should be at least 200-300 words. Include: training background, artistic philosophy, exhibitions, collections, press mentions. Naturally incorporate keywords like "figurative painter," "oil on panel," "Tel Aviv studio." Mention specific galleries, museums, or notable collectors if applicable.

### Artist Statement
Consider adding a short artist statement section. Collectors and curators look for this. It helps with searches like "figurative art philosophy" and "contemporary realism artist statement."

### Exhibition History
If Mark has exhibition history, adding a list of exhibitions (even as part of the About page) adds credibility and creates keyword-rich content that galleries search for.

### Blog (Optional but Valuable)
A blog section with 1-2 posts per month would significantly boost SEO over time. Topics that drive art collector traffic: behind-the-scenes studio process, work-in-progress documentation, thoughts on exhibitions or art fairs, technique discussions (e.g., "Why I paint on panel instead of canvas"). Each post creates a new indexed page and targets long-tail keywords.

## Social Media for SEO

### Instagram (Most Important for Artists)
Post 3-5 times per week. Always include markglezin.com in bio. Use relevant hashtags: #figurativeart, #oilpainting, #contemporaryart, #telavivart, #portraitpainting. Instagram doesn't directly boost SEO, but it drives traffic to the site, which signals Google that the site is valuable.

### Pinterest (Underrated for Art SEO)
Pin 5-10 paintings per week to boards organized by category (Portraits, Landscapes, etc.). Pinterest images rank in Google Image search. Each pin should link back to the works page. This is a direct SEO multiplier for visual artists.

### Facebook
The existing Facebook page (MarkGlezinART) is good. Share new works and link to the website. Facebook business pages get indexed by Google.

## Monthly SEO Checklist (2-3 Hours)

1. **Check Google Search Console** — Look at which queries bring impressions and clicks. Note any crawl errors and fix them.

2. **Review search performance** — Which paintings or pages get the most image search traffic? Create more content around those themes.

3. **Add new work** — Run build.py after adding new paintings. Each new painting is a new indexed image in Google.

4. **Social media consistency** — Post to Instagram 3-5x/week, pin to Pinterest 5-10x/week.

5. **One backlink action** — Reach out to one art blog, submit to one directory, or comment meaningfully on an art publication. Quality over quantity.

---

## Quarterly Review

Every 3 months, review in Google Search Console: total impressions and clicks trend (should be growing), top-performing keywords and pages, any new opportunities from "impressions but low clicks" queries (these need better meta descriptions), and mobile usability issues.

## Realistic Timeline

**Month 1-2:** Google indexes the site. Initial impressions start appearing in Search Console. Image search begins showing paintings.

**Month 3-6:** Keyword rankings begin stabilizing. Art marketplace profiles drive referral traffic and backlink authority. Social media traffic grows.

**Month 6-12:** Established presence in art-related searches. Long-tail keywords start converting. Gallery and curator inquiries increase.

**Key insight:** SEO for art is a long game. The technical foundation is now solid. The ongoing work (social media, marketplace profiles, occasional content) is what compounds over time into significant traffic from collectors and galleries worldwide.
